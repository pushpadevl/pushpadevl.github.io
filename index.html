About Me

I am Pushpendra, a cryptogrpaphy student, a part-time photographer and the creator of this website. I do various other things to keep my life interesting but there is something omnipresent among all these things, it's the bug of curiosity and the will to always learn something new, not just academically but in every way my body allows.

To affix my academic standing, I have a Bachelors in Electronics and Communications Engineering, Master of Science in Economics and just recently, Master of Cryptology and Security. The first two degrees were offered by Birla Institute of Technology and Science Pilani, while the last one was offered by Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata. Now I wouldn't say that the first choice of choosing an integrated course (at BITS) was the right decision, becasue with my marks, I could have gotten any 4 year course as well, but Computer Science. Although I did make up for my shortcomings and eventually did learn a lot of Computer science and later went to Kolkata to study Cryptology. I will take that as a win. At ISI, I had something I had never experienced before, the autonomy along with no financial worries and best of all, people who share a similar thought process as mine.

Apart from the academics, I do a lot of other stuff such as capturing photographs of basically anything that has naturally arrived on Earth. I don't discriminate and thus I don't put myself a specific clique of photographers. Another thing that excites me are acrobatics and plyometrics, which I think is the ultimate form of human body mastery. I don't workout to maintain my body or something, which many people is the motivation behind it; I do it just to see if I can. I do it for fun, it gives me the instant gratificaiton of doing somehting perfectly. I remember learning to kip up, and I had not learned it until the third year of college, when during a winter vacation, I started doing it daily for an hour, and I did it. The feeling of ecstasy was unforgettable. Then I learned how to do a roundabout with a football, and catch a football mid-air on my foot, and several other tricks. For this, I would keep doing this daily for about 10 hours and I eventually learned it. Currently, I have almost learned how to d a handstand, having done it for 7 seconds on camera. I learned to do the infamous Yogi-frog pose. There are several others.
